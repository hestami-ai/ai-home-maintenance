    *   **Source Requirements**: REQ-9, REQ-10

### Defined Workflows

*   **WF-MANAGE-CORE-ENTITY**: Handles CRUD operations for core data model entities.
*   **WF-USER-LOGIN**: Authenticates a user and establishes a session.
*   **WF-MANAGE-USER-ROLE**: Allows an administrator to assign or revoke roles for a user.
*   **WF-POST-TO-GL**: Posts a financial transaction to the General Ledger.
*   **WF-POST-PERIODIC-ASSESSMENT**: Scheduled workflow to automatically post recurring assessments.
*   **WF-PROCESS-WORK-ORDER-PAYMENT**: Manages payment for a completed work order.
*   **WF-MANAGE-VIOLATION**: Handles the lifecycle for a community violation, including notices, hearings, and fines.
*   **WF-PROCESS-ARC-REQUEST**: Manages the submission, review, and decision process for architectural requests.
*   **WF-PROCESS-DOCUMENT-AI**: Analyzes an uploaded document using AI for extraction, classification, and summarization.
*   **WF-MOBILE-OFFLINE-SYNC**: Synchronizes data between the native mobile client and the server for offline-first capabilities.

## Approved Plan (with entity/domain proposals)
This is a summary of the approved plan, which guides the architectural design.

*   **Goal**: To build a three-pillar platform (Home Assistant, Service Provider FSM, Community Association Management) for property management, maintenance, and governance.
*   **Core Requirements**:
    *   **REQ-1**: Support a common data model (associations, properties, people, accounting, etc.).
    *   **REQ-2**: Provide an auditable General Ledger (GL).
    *   **REQ-4**: Integrate work orders with Accounts Payable.
    *   **REQ-5 & REQ-6**: Implement workflows for violations and architectural requests (ARC).
    *   **REQ-7**: Provide role-based portals for Owners, Tenants, Managers, and Vendors.
    *   **REQ-8**: Feature AI-driven document analysis.
    *   **REQ-9 & REQ-10**: Provide native, offline-first mobile clients.
*   **Key Decisions**:
    *   **DEC-1**: A three-phased development approach corresponding to the three pillars.
    *   **DEC-4**: A pre-defined tech stack: SvelteKit (Frontend), Node.js/Bun (Backend), DBOS (Workflow), PostgreSQL (DB), and Cerbos (AuthZ).
*   **Key Constraints**:
    *   **CON-2**: Durable, multi-step processes must be implemented as DBOS versioned workflows.
    *   **CON-3**: Strict multi-tenant data isolation must be enforced via PostgreSQL RLS.
*   **Proposed Domains & Entities**: The plan includes extensive proposals for 15 domains (e.g., Property Management, Financial Management, AI & Automation) and 31 associated entities (e.g., Property, Service Request, Invoice, Violation). These form the basis of the common data model.
