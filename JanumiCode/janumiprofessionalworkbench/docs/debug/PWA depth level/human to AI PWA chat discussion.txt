## The key terminology

This PWA defines a **work decomposition**, not an authoritative workflow:

- **Composition edges** say what the undertaking is made of.
- **Artifact hand-offs** indicate that one work unit’s output can satisfy another’s input.
- An **Execution Plan/Workflow**, which is not authored here, would determine exact ordering, concurrency, dependencies, gates, and dates.

Nevertheless, the artifact hand-offs provide a strong **intended lifecycle narrative**.

## Likely undertaking flow

### 1. Originate the need

**Business & Service Need Origination** establishes why a data center initiative exists: business drivers, service mission, constraints, desired outcomes, sponsorship, and triggering conditions.

Output:

`originating-business-and-service-need`

### 2. Define the strategy and program

That need feeds **Strategic Definition, Predesign & Programming**, composed of:

1. **Business Need, Stakeholders & Capacity Strategy**
   - Defines workload, growth, availability, sustainability, budget, schedule, and decision criteria.
2. **Site, Utility & Environmental Feasibility**
   - Tests sites, utilities, climate, water, hazards, connectivity, and permitting.
3. **Owner’s Project Requirements & Commissioning Strategy**
   - Converts the strategic and feasibility findings into measurable OPR, acceptance criteria, and a commissioning plan.

Principal output:

`approved-data-center-program`

The artifact chain suggests the internal progression:

**Need → strategic scope → feasibility → OPR and commissioning strategy**

Some of this work could overlap in a real execution plan.

### 3. Design the facility

The approved program initiates **Design, Construction & Commissioning**.

First, **Basis of Design & Coordinated Facility Design** consumes the OPR and feasibility findings and develops:

- `basis-of-design`
- `issued-design-documents`

### 4. Procure, construct, and install

**Procurement, Construction & Installation** consumes the issued design and commissioning plan. It procures and builds the facility, performs inspections and startup, and produces:

- `installed-and-started-systems`
- `construction-quality-record`

### 5. Commission and test integrated performance

**Commissioning Verification & Integrated Systems Testing** evaluates the installed systems against the OPR, Basis of Design, and commissioning plan.

It produces:

- `commissioning-evidence`
- `systems-manual`
- `commissioned-facility-baseline`

So the intended capital-delivery narrative is:

**OPR → design → construction/startup → commissioning and integrated testing**

### 6. Hand over into live service

**Handover, Training & Service Readiness** consumes commissioning evidence, the systems manual, and construction quality records.

It completes training, procedures, asset data, spares, warranties, and readiness decisions, producing:

- `accepted-operational-baseline`
- `trained-operations-readiness`

### 7. Operate, maintain, and monitor

**Operate, Maintain & Monitor** uses that accepted baseline and readiness evidence to run the facility.

It produces an accumulating history:

- `operating-and-maintenance-records`
- `operational-performance-history`

This child has cardinality **M+**, meaning an undertaking expects **one or more** operational work units rather than one monolithic operation. These might represent operating periods, facilities, service epochs, or another partition selected by the eventual execution plan.

### 8. Apply operational feedback loops

Two kinds of work can recur when applicable:

- **Performance Analysis & Optimization** — conditional and repeatable when measurements justify improvements.
- **Recommissioning & Ongoing Commissioning** — conditional and repeatable when performance drifts, major changes occur, recurring faults appear, or periodic assurance is required.

Conceptually:

**Operate → measure → analyze/tune/recommission → continue operating**

The PWA expresses their applicability and inputs, but does not fully encode the return loop into operations as an authoritative workflow.

### 9. Plan and deliver major lifecycle changes

Operational history can trigger **Renewal, Adaptation & End-of-Life** work.

When justified:

1. **Expansion, Modernization & Renewal Planning**
   - Creates an `approved-lifecycle-change-program`.
2. **Retrofit, Expansion & Technology Refresh Delivery**
   - Delivers the approved change while protecting live-service continuity.
   - Produces a `modernized-facility-baseline`.

Both are conditional and repeatable, so there may be multiple modernization cycles over the facility’s life.

A realistic execution plan would likely route major changes back through appropriate design, testing, commissioning, handover, and operations activities. That loop is not explicitly established by this PWA.

### 10. Decommission and close

**Decommissioning, Disposition & Site Closure** is mandatory exactly once in the current decomposition. It governs service exit, isolation, removal, data-bearing assets, environmental disposition, salvage, retention, and closure or repurposing.

It produces:

- `retirement-and-disposition-record`
- `data-center-lifecycle-record`

That lifecycle record is the root undertaking’s final output.

## Condensed lifecycle story

> **Originate the business need → define strategy and feasibility → establish OPR and commissioning strategy → design → procure and construct → commission and test → hand over and train → operate and monitor → repeatedly optimize, recommission, or modernize when needed → decommission and preserve the lifecycle record.**

## What happens to each individual work unit

Each instantiated PWU also has its own generic RPH lifecycle, approximately:

**Proposed → Shaping → Ready → Planned → Executing → Evidence Pending → Under Assurance → Satisfied → Recomposed if required → Baselined**

Important implications:

- Execution success alone is insufficient.
- Required outputs and evidence must exist.
- Required assurance must be satisfied before the work is considered satisfied.
- Child results may be recomposed into their parent’s result.
- Authorized promotion establishes a baseline.
- Work can also become blocked, challenged, reshaped, rejected, invalidated, abandoned, or superseded.

That topology is **derived and simulation-only**. It is not the persisted state of this PWA or of a running undertaking.

### Interpretation A: The undertaking creates the need internally

The plan would specify:

```text
1. Execute “Business & Service Need Origination”.
2. Produce and approve `originating-business-and-service-need`.
3. Bind that artifact to:
   - the root’s required input;

```

The root instance can exist as the enclosing undertaking before that artifact is produced, but the plan would state that its substantive input-dependent execution—or a specified readiness milestone—is gated by the child’s output.

### Interpretation B: The need must come from outside

The plan would instead specify:

```text
Entry condition:
An authorized `originating-business-and-service-need` must be supplied by
portfolio governance, corporate strategy, or another preceding undertaking.

Before execution:
Validate and bind that external artifact to the root and its consumers.
```

In that interpretation, the mandatory **Business & Service Need Origination** child is awkward because it appears to produce something already required externally. The plan would have to say that the child validates, formalizes, or reauthorizes the external need rather than originating it from nothing.

### What “resolve the boundary” means

The boundary is the line between:

- **information supplied before this undertaking begins**, and
- **information produced as part of this undertaking**.

An Execution Plan resolves it by establishing an explicit rule such as:

> “The root may be instantiated without the approved need, but Strategic Definition cannot begin until Business & Service Need Origination has produced and authorized it.”

Or:

> “The approved need is an entry prerequisite supplied externally; the undertaking cannot enter execution without it.”

The current matching input/output names do **not** establish either rule. They only show that the child’s output is compatible with the root’s input. Composition also does not establish “child runs before parent.”

A cleaner reusable model might distinguish two artifacts:

```text
External input:  `business-or-service-trigger`
Origination output: `authorized-business-and-service-need`
```

Then the child consumes the external trigger and produces the authorized need used by the root and downstream work. That would reduce the ambiguity in the PWA itself, rather than leaving every Execution Plan to interpret it.
