# 1. Purpose

This Roadmap defines the master implementation path for migrating legacy JanumiCode from its hardcoded phase-oriented architecture to the canonical Janumi model:

```text
Janumi Platform
└── Janumi Professional Workbench (JPWB)
    └── JanumiCode
        └── Product Realization PWA
            └── Product Realization Undertakings
```

It governs:

- the capability states that SHALL be established;
- their architectural dependency order;
- the evidence required before advancement;
- the constraints that implementation SHALL preserve;
- the authority boundaries surrounding material decisions;
- the process by which a repository-connected coding agent SHALL generate repository-specific detailed implementation roadmaps.

This Roadmap deliberately does **not** attempt to predict every file, class, database migration, adapter, refactor, pull request, or task from outside the repository. Those details SHALL be derived by an intelligent coding agent or human engineering team with direct access to:

1. the canonical normative corpus;
2. this Master Roadmap;
3. the actual repository and its revision history;
4. current tests, persistence, and runtime evidence.

The resulting repository-specific detailed roadmaps are controlled derivative artifacts of this Master Roadmap.

# 2. Operating model

```text
Canonical normative corpus
        ↓
Master Normative Implementation Roadmap
        ↓
Repository-connected investigation and reasoning
        ↓
Repository-specific Detailed Implementation Roadmap
        ↓
Implementation work packages and task plans
        ↓
Code, migrations, tests, and operational changes
        ↓
Evidence and conformance review
        ↓
Wave gate and next detailed roadmap
```

The repository investigation is therefore not a prerequisite missing from this Roadmap. It is implementation work required by this Roadmap. Its output is the code-grounded plan through which the applicable master wave is executed.

# 3. Normative language

- **SHALL / SHALL NOT** — mandatory for conformance.
- **SHOULD / SHOULD NOT** — expected unless a documented and approved reason justifies divergence.
- **MAY** — permitted but not required.
- **INFORMATIVE** — explanatory and not independently binding.

A work package is not normatively complete merely because code exists. Completion requires implementation evidence and conformance to the applicable normative outcome.

# 5. Source authority in brownfield implementation

| Question | Primary authority |
| --- | --- |
| What does legacy JanumiCode actually do? | Source code, tests, persisted state, and observed execution traces. |
| What was legacy behavior intended to accomplish? | Existing documentation interpreted with code-grounded evidence. |
| What must the replacement become? | The approved canonical normative corpus. |
| Which legacy behavior must survive? | Code-grounded classification approved under the applicable wave. |
| Which accidental behavior may be removed? | Explicit implementation or architecture decision supported by evidence. |
| Which policy, security, legal, or authority constraints apply? | The applicable governing source and authority. |

The coding agent SHALL treat the repository as authoritative evidence of current state, but SHALL NOT allow accidental implementation history to silently redefine the approved target. It SHALL treat the normative corpus as the approved target, but SHALL NOT pretend that the corpus is infallible concerning brownfield facts.

Where material conflict exists, the agent SHALL:

1. identify the conflict;
2. collect supporting evidence;
3. classify its impact;
4. propose whether code, documentation, roadmap, or multiple artifacts should change;
5. update the applicable divergence or decision record;
6. obtain authority when a material-decision trigger applies.

# 7. Program mission and completion condition

The program SHALL establish JanumiCode as the software-product specialization of JPWB, operating the Product Realization PWA through the RPH, while safely migrating legacy JanumiCode and proving that JPWB can author and evolve the PWA that JanumiCode itself uses.

Program completion requires:

- RPH as the sole semantic authority for new Product Realization Undertakings;
- the complete Product Realization PWA from intent through Product Baseline Promotion;
- legacy phases reduced to derived compatibility milestones;
- JPWB capable of defining, validating, publishing, and evolving the Product Realization PWA;
- tenant PWA derivation and Product Shape Package export;
- JanumiCode production integration with Janumi Platform tenancy, authority, billing, deployment, and operations;
- no unresolved critical architectural, migration, security, or semantic-integrity divergence.

# 8. Mandatory architectural rules

1. Every Undertaking SHALL have one writable semantic authority.
2. PWA definitions, Undertakings, Professional Work Graphs, Execution Plans, Execution Workflows, and Views SHALL remain distinct.
3. Execution success SHALL NOT imply assurance satisfaction, PWU satisfaction, or baseline acceptance.
4. Material assumptions SHALL be explicit and traceable.
5. Material decomposition SHALL allocate obligations and constraints and define recomposition.
6. Claims SHALL be supported by admissible evidence before authoritative acceptance.
7. Assessments, decisions, waivers, and baselines SHALL bind exact semantic versions.
8. Published PWAs and authoritative baselines SHALL be immutable.
9. UI and canvas state SHALL remain derived presentation.
10. PWA definitions SHALL NOT grant runtime authority.
11. Legacy phases SHALL become derived compatibility projections before full cutover.
12. Repository commits and deployments SHALL remain distinct from professional baselines.
13. PWA changes SHALL NOT silently mutate existing Undertakings.
14. The agent SHALL surface material conflicts rather than silently resolve them.
15. The agent SHALL be empowered to revise implementation plans and propose normative corrections when evidence warrants them.

# 9. Repository-connected coding-agent mandate

For each activated wave, the coding agent SHALL:

1. read the applicable corpus in canonical order;
2. identify the exact repository revision and relevant runtime environment;
3. inspect current code, tests, persistence, prompts, context assembly, validators, integrations, and operational behavior relevant to the wave;
4. distinguish confirmed findings, inferences, assumptions, and unresolved questions;
5. classify legacy elements as `PRESERVE`, `RECLASSIFY`, `GENERALIZE`, `REPLACE`, `REMOVE`, `DEFER`, or `UNRESOLVED` where applicable;
6. generate a normalized Detailed Implementation Roadmap conforming to `JAN-ROADMAP-001-A`;
7. critique that roadmap against the corpus, repository evidence, migration safety, security, and maintainability;
8. surface material decisions and divergences;
9. execute the approved or otherwise authorized detailed roadmap methodically;
10. maintain traceability from normative obligation to code, test, and evidence;
11. update the detailed roadmap when discoveries change the route without silently changing the destination;
12. produce the gate package and proposed next-wave roadmap when the wave is complete.

The coding agent MAY continue through approved work without requesting repeated task-by-task confirmation. It SHALL pause for a material-decision trigger or a wave gate.

# 10. Material-decision triggers

The coding agent SHALL obtain explicit authority before:

- materially changing the approved target architecture;
- removing behavior classified for preservation;
- weakening an invariant, assurance policy, security control, or authority boundary;
- making an irreversible or high-risk migration choice not already authorized;
- transferring semantic authority from legacy state to RPH;
- publishing or materially changing a PWA version;
- promoting an authoritative production baseline;
- accepting an unresolved critical divergence;
- materially expanding or contracting program scope;
- committing the organization to a new platform dependency with substantial operational or commercial consequence.

Ordinary implementation choices within the approved architecture MAY be made by the coding agent and documented in the detailed roadmap or ADRs.

# 15. Detailed-roadmap generation requirement

Every wave SHALL begin with a repository-grounded Detailed Implementation Roadmap generated under `JAN-ROADMAP-001-A`.

The detailed roadmap SHALL not be a generic restatement of the master work packages. It SHALL identify:

- current repository facts and evidence;
- target semantic mapping;
- alternatives and selected strategy;
- concrete repository changes;
- migration and compatibility behavior;
- tests and evidence;
- risks, decisions, and unresolved matters;
- implementation ordering and rollback.

For W0, the coding agent SHALL first perform enough reconnaissance to generate a competent investigation roadmap. Execution of W0 SHALL then produce the code-grounded evidence and the proposed W1 Detailed Implementation Roadmap as part of the G0 package.

# 16. Agent autonomy within an activated wave

Once a Detailed Implementation Roadmap is authorized, the agent SHALL proceed methodically through its work packages without waiting for repeated prompts such as “proceed” or “what is next,” provided that:

- no material-decision trigger occurs;
- no critical contradiction invalidates the plan;
- security or data safety does not require interruption;
- the work remains within the activated wave and approved scope.

The agent SHALL update progress, evidence, discoveries, and roadmap revisions as work proceeds. It SHALL not claim completion when evidence is absent.

# 17. Gate protocol

At each gate, the agent or implementation team SHALL provide:

- master and detailed work-package status;
- code-grounded findings and deviations;
- test and evidence results;
- migration, recovery, security, and operational evidence;
- decisions, deferrals, waivers, and divergences;
- residual risk;
- a recommendation: `APPROVE`, `APPROVE_WITH_CONDITIONS`, `REJECT`, or `DEFER`;
- the proposed Detailed Implementation Roadmap for the next wave where sufficient evidence exists.

A conditional approval SHALL identify exact conditions, authority, deadline or triggering event, consequence, and required evidence.

# 18. Living control registers

The program SHALL maintain lean, machine-readable registers for:

- architecture and implementation decisions;
- divergences between corpus, repository, roadmap, fixture, tests, and implementation;
- deferrals and waivers;
- assumptions and unresolved questions;
- normative-to-implementation traceability.

The registers support implementation judgment; they SHALL not become an administrative substitute for engineering work.

# 19. Prohibited shortcuts

- Treating the canvas as the canonical ontology.
- Treating prompts or phases as PWU identity.
- Maintaining two writable semantic state machines.
- Equating execution completion with assurance or professional satisfaction.
- Storing validator prose as authoritative assurance without canonical structure.
- Allowing stale assessments or approvals to govern changed semantic versions.
- Hiding material assumptions in generated prose.
- Treating a commit or deployment as a baseline automatically.
- Editing published PWAs or authoritative baselines in place.
- Granting runtime capabilities through PWA definition.
- Silently propagating PWA changes into existing Undertakings.
- Blindly retrying uncertain external side effects.
- Inventing repository facts or evidence to make a roadmap appear complete.
- Freezing a detailed roadmap when code-grounded evidence shows that it must change.

# 20. Master-roadmap change control

A successor Master Roadmap revision is required when changing:

- wave order;
- major capability outcome;
- architectural boundary;
- gate criteria;
- semantic-authority strategy;
- scope of PWA, JPWB, or Janumi Platform productization;
- mandatory invariant or prohibited shortcut.

Repository-specific route changes normally update the Detailed Implementation Roadmap rather than this Master Roadmap.

# 21. Calendar and staffing

Wave order and evidence gates are normative. Dates, staffing, and effort estimates are informative until grounded against the repository and approved separately. A schedule change SHALL NOT weaken conformance criteria.
