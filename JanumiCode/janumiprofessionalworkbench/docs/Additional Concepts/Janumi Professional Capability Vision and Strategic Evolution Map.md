The current Janumi canonical documentation, generated contracts, accepted architecture decisions, and executable conformance tests remain authoritative for implementation. In particular, this vision does not alter the established boundaries among Professional Work Architectures, Undertakings, PWU Types, PWU Instances, the Recursive Professional Harness, Assurance Engineering, Decisions, or Baselines.

They should influence implementation only after deliberate semantic design, governance, contract evolution, migration planning, and conformance work.

The ambition of a vision is to show where a system may ultimately lead. The discipline of this vision is to distinguish that destination from what exists today.

## 1. The grand vision

Janumi begins from a simple but far-reaching observation:

> Professional work is not fundamentally a sequence of tasks or a collection of artifacts. It is the exercise of professional capability within a changing, uncertain, consequential reality.

Software, contracts, filings, diagnoses, construction schedules, rockets, lithography machines, particle accelerators, and public infrastructure are visible outputs. Beneath each is a professional system: people, institutions, knowledge, methods, tools, authority, relationships, judgment, memory, and coordinated work accumulated over time.

The enduring asset is not only the output. It is the capability to produce, sustain, adapt, validate, and reproduce valuable outcomes under changing conditions.

Janumi therefore aspires to become more than an AI agent platform, workflow system, project-management product, or artifact generator. Its long-term purpose is:

> To provide infrastructure through which humans and agents can deliberately shape, exercise, assure, preserve, and evolve professional capability across complex scenarios, organizational boundaries, technological change, and generations of time.

At the practical frontier, this means helping a team turn an intended outcome into a coherent Professional Work Architecture and execute it without losing Intent, Evidence, authority, or accountability.

At the civilizational frontier, it means helping professional ecosystems preserve the ability to do things that no individual understands completely, no single document specifies, no single organization controls, and no single generation can sustain alone.

That frontier includes capabilities such as:

- manufacturing leading-edge semiconductors;
- operating safe global aviation;
- building and maintaining particle accelerators;
- developing vaccines and clinical systems;
- creating and operating advanced energy infrastructure;
- constructing resilient cities;
- designing and sustaining deep-space missions;
- preserving the rule of law across changing institutions;
- maintaining the knowledge and coordination necessary to build machines such as ASML's EUV systems, CERN, Starship, and the James Webb Space Telescope.

Janumi's ultimate concern is not simply whether an artifact was produced. It is whether humanity remains capable of producing, understanding, governing, maintaining, improving, and, when necessary, reconstituting the outcomes on which society depends.

## 2. Why professional work requires a different foundation

### 2.1 Complex wholeness

Conventional work systems usually assume that a difficult objective can be decomposed into tasks, assigned, completed, and reassembled. That approach is effective for many complicated problems. It becomes inadequate when decomposition loses relationships essential to the whole.

Professional work carries meaning that does not naturally survive reduction:

- original Intent;
- cross-functional obligations;
- unstated but consequential Assumptions;
- human and institutional authority;
- stakeholder expectations;
- legal, ethical, safety, and regulatory Constraints;
- relationships among artifacts and decisions;
- uncertainty and alternative interpretations;
- historical circumstances;
- physical and organizational dependencies;
- interaction effects among otherwise correct parts;
- the difference between producing an output and changing reality.

This is the practical meaning of the **irreversibility of reductionism**: understanding or completing every part does not guarantee recovery of the whole.

Janumi answers that failure through explicit decomposition Claims, obligation allocation, recomposition, Intent preservation, Evidence, Assurance Engineering, continuous observation, impact propagation, and governed reconciliation.

### 2.2 Outcomes are changes in reality

A source-code commit is not a successful product. A signed contract is not a successful merger. A diagnostic dossier is not a recovered patient. A completed construction schedule is not a safe building. A launched spacecraft is not a sustained exploration capability.

Artifacts are professionally important because they preserve, communicate, authorize, constrain, or evidence work. They are not the Outcome itself.

Janumi must therefore maintain a disciplined distinction:

```text
artifact produced
≠ professional obligation satisfied
≠ parent outcome recomposed
≠ authorized acceptance
≠ real-world outcome achieved
≠ capability demonstrated for future scenarios
```

### 2.3 Time is constitutive

Professional reality cannot be represented adequately without time.

What occurred, what was observed, what was known, what was recorded, what became effective, what remained valid, what was superseded, and what was predicted may all have different times.

Professional capability is also temporal. It can emerge, mature, degrade, become unavailable, be transferred, become obsolete, adapt, enter dormancy, or be reconstituted. An organization that was capable ten years ago may no longer be capable. A capability that cannot be exercised today may remain recoverable. An outcome may not be observable until decades after the work that produced it.

Time is therefore not metadata attached to professional state. It is one of the dimensions through which professional meaning, Evidence, authority, continuity, and capability must be understood.

## 3. The Janumi regenerative cycle

The full vision is better understood as a cycle than as a top-down hierarchy:

```text
Civilizational knowledge, institutions, and accumulated practice
        ↓ enable
Professional Systems ── realize ── Professional Capabilities
                                             ↓ applied to
Professional Scenarios ── require ───────────┘
        ↓ analyze, frame, and shape
Professional Work Architectures
        ↓ instantiate
Undertakings and Professional Work Graphs
        ↓ recursively perform
Professional Work Units
        ↓ through
Human + agent + tool + external-system execution
        ↓ produce
Evidence + Decisions + Actions + Artifacts
        ↓ affect
Outcomes and changed reality
        ↓ generate
Scenario reconciliation + capability Evidence + governed learning
        └───────────────────────────────────────────────↺
```

Each layer answers a different question:

| Concept | Primary question |
|---|---|
| **Civilizational knowledge** | What accumulated understanding, practice, culture, standards, and institutions enable society to act? |
| **Professional System** | What socio-technical arrangement currently bears and realizes capability? |
| **Professional Capability** | What can that system demonstrably and legitimately accomplish, under which conditions, across time? |
| **Professional Scenario** | What consequential situation exists, what is believed about it, and what futures remain possible? |
| **Shape Engineering** | How should professional capability be organized so that it can act coherently in scenarios of this class? |
| **Professional Work Architecture** | What reusable, executable work architecture expresses that shape? |
| **Undertaking** | What exact body of professional work has been committed to this situation? |
| **Professional Work Unit** | What bounded professional obligation is being discharged? |
| **Recursive Professional Harness** | How is recursive work coordinated, supervised, reconciled, recomposed, recovered, and escalated? |
| **Assurance Engineering** | What Claims are justified by admissible Evidence and may safely cross protected boundaries? |
| **Narrative Memory** | How should the evolution and meaning of the trajectory be interpreted and learned from? |

The cycle is regenerative because Outcomes change the scenario, experience changes the Evidence for capability, and governed learning changes what future professional systems can do.

## 4. Professional Scenario

### 4.1 Candidate definition

> A Professional Scenario is an identity-bearing, time-evolving, epistemically qualified, authority- and access-bounded representation of a consequential situation across its relevant past, present, and possible future states. It relates actors, environments, observed and asserted state, desired and possible futures, Constraints, uncertainty, Evidence, Decisions, and linked Undertakings while remaining explicitly distinct from external reality.

A Professional Scenario is the situated side of professional work. It is the condition to which capability must respond.

### 4.2 Why it is distinct

A scenario is not reusable in the manner of a PWA. It is not bounded work in the manner of an Undertaking. It is not a sequence in the manner of a Plan. It is not merely an interpretive history in the manner of Narrative Memory.

One scenario may sponsor multiple Undertakings, invoke multiple PWAs, require multiple professional systems, and persist after particular work has completed.

A complex international merger, an unresolved longitudinal clinical case, the construction of a major hospital, the modernization of a national power grid, or a multigenerational space program may each require many bodies of professional work while remaining one evolving consequential situation.

### 4.3 Essential properties

#### Identity and continuity

A scenario has durable identity even while its representation changes. The emergence of new Evidence, a revised objective, a regulatory intervention, a failed Assumption, or a new possible future does not create a wholly unrelated situation. Its prior states remain reconstructable.

#### Temporal depth

A scenario relates:

- relevant history;
- current observed and believed state;
- prior Decisions and their effective periods;
- changing Constraints;
- anticipated Events;
- possible futures;
- outcome horizons;
- unresolved long-term obligations.

It distinguishes event time, observation time, knowledge time, record time, effective time, validity time, and supersession where professional meaning requires those distinctions.

#### Epistemic qualification

A scenario never presents all content as equally true. It distinguishes:

- direct Observations;
- admitted Evidence;
- asserted and contested Claims;
- Assumptions;
- hypotheses;
- predictions;
- unresolved Questions;
- desired states;
- prohibited states;
- inaccessible or missing information.

The scenario remains honest about what is unknown, disputed, stale, inaccessible, or inferred.

#### Authority and compartmentation

A scenario may cross many authority domains without giving any actor unrestricted access to the whole. Its projections are conditioned by tenant, organization, client, matter, workstream, legal or professional privilege, ethical wall, jurisdiction, purpose, time, and delegated authority.

There may be no single omniscient view. Janumi must be able to preserve relationships among partially visible objects without disclosing their protected contents.

#### Consequence and intervention

Not every situation merits scenario identity. A Professional Scenario exists when a situation has sufficient continuity, consequence, uncertainty, professional responsibility, and potential intervention to sponsor or materially affect one or more Undertakings.

### 4.4 Candidate semantic contents

A Professional Scenario would relate, rather than necessarily own:

1. scenario identity, framing, scope, custodians, and sensitivity;
2. actors, organizations, institutions, agent proxies, interests, affiliations, and authority over time;
3. observed, asserted, disputed, and unknown state;
4. Intent, desired Outcomes, unacceptable Outcomes, and alternative futures;
5. applicable laws, standards, methods, policies, and historical knowledge;
6. Claims, Evidence, Assumptions, contradictions, Questions, and uncertainty;
7. capability demand, capability gaps, urgency, risk, and required assurance;
8. relevant PWAs, Undertakings, PWUs, Decisions, Actions, Baselines, and Outcomes;
9. federated repositories and authoritative external sources;
10. Events, material revisions, impact propagation, and prior scenario versions.

The scenario should be a governed semantic structure, not one giant record or agent prompt.

### 4.5 Non-equivalences

```text
Professional Scenario ≠ external reality
Professional Scenario ≠ PWA
Professional Scenario ≠ Undertaking
Professional Scenario ≠ Professional Work Graph
Professional Scenario ≠ plan or workflow
Professional Scenario ≠ case-file repository
Professional Scenario ≠ complete digital twin
Professional Scenario ≠ Narrative Memory
Professional Scenario ≠ LLM context window
```

### 4.6 Evolution rather than completion

A scenario may be recognized, framed, revised, branched into possible futures, related to sub-scenarios, challenged, made dormant, reactivated, transformed, succeeded, or archived. These are conceptual operations, not proposed wire states.

Scenario closure must never imply that reality has been permanently resolved. A completed merger can create years of regulatory obligations. A completed construction project becomes an operated facility. A diagnostic investigation may produce a treatment path whose outcome remains uncertain. A completed launch begins a mission.

### 4.7 Scenario assurance

Assurance over a Professional Scenario would address:

- source identity, provenance, and admissibility;
- temporal consistency and freshness;
- correct epistemic classification;
- contradiction preservation;
- material omission and coverage;
- unauthorized inference or disclosure;
- compartment and privilege enforcement;
- faithful generation of scenario projections;
- scenario-to-Undertaking impact closure;
- premature elimination of possible futures;
- invalidation caused by changed conditions.

Every material agent-produced scenario transformation remains subject to the universal de minimis assurance floor, including Reasoning Review.

## 5. Professional System and Professional Capability

### 5.1 Professional System

> A Professional System is the socio-technical arrangement of people, organizations, knowledge, institutions, tools, infrastructure, relationships, authority, work architectures, and governance through which Professional Capabilities are realized.

The bearer of capability is not a document, model, PWA, or agent. It is a professional system.

A leading law firm's merger capability may depend on partners, associates, local counsel, economists, professional methods, regulator relationships, privilege controls, agents, evidence systems, client personnel, domain PWAs, and institutional reputation.

The capability to manufacture an EUV lithography system extends across firms, suppliers, universities, customers, standards bodies, laboratories, infrastructure, regulators, field-service organizations, and accumulated industrial practice.

### 5.2 Candidate definition of Professional Capability

> A Professional Capability is an evidence-backed, versioned, temporally qualified, and governable ability of a Professional System or federation of systems to transform a defined class of Professional Scenarios toward intended Outcomes under declared conditions, authority, Constraints, risk, and assurance, with demonstrated repeatability, adaptability, and resilience.

Capability is the ability side of professional work. It answers not merely what a provider claims to offer, but what a professional system can justifiably be expected to accomplish.

### 5.3 Capability is conditional

No professional capability exists without conditions.

A firm may be capable of conducting cross-border mergers in certain industries and jurisdictions, at certain levels of complexity, when conflicts permit representation, qualified professionals are available, secure infrastructure can be used, and required local authority can be obtained.

A healthcare system may be capable of performing a diagnostic investigation for a class of cases while lacking authority, Evidence, or infrastructure for another.

A manufacturing ecosystem may retain knowledge of a product while lacking a critical supplier, material, facility, or generation of experienced practitioners necessary to reproduce it.

### 5.4 Capability, competence, capacity, and authority

| Concept | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Capability** | Demonstrated ability of a professional system to produce a class of Outcomes. |
| **Competence** | Knowledge, skill, or qualification of a contributor. |
| **Capacity** | Amount of work the system can currently support. |
| **Availability** | Whether the capability can be supplied now. |
| **Authority** | Whether an actor may lawfully or professionally act or decide. |
| **Readiness** | Whether capability can be mobilized for this exact scenario under current conditions. |
| **Performance** | What occurred when capability was exercised. |
| **Maturity** | How developed, repeatable, measured, governed, and resilient the capability is. |

A capable organization may lack current capacity. A competent person may lack authority. An available agent may be inapplicable to the scenario. A mature PWA may be unusable because a critical provider is unavailable.

### 5.5 Capability facets

A Professional Capability should be describable through:

1. **Outcome class** — what result it can pursue and what it does not claim to produce;
2. **scenario applicability** — supported conditions, jurisdictions, scale, complexity, risk, and known exclusions;
3. **professional-system requirements** — constituent people, organizations, infrastructure, knowledge, tools, and relationships;
4. **work realization** — applicable PWAs, methods, techniques, profiles, and permitted adaptations;
5. **authority and qualification** — licensing, accreditation, standing, delegation, and restrictions;
6. **assurance regime** — policies, independence, Evidence, protected transitions, and non-waivable controls;
7. **performance Evidence** — relevant Undertakings, Outcomes, failures, limitations, and observation periods;
8. **reliability and maturity** — repeatability, variance, known failure modes, and residual uncertainty;
9. **adaptability** — demonstrated ability to respond to novel or changing scenarios without abandoning essential obligations;
10. **resilience** — tolerance for personnel turnover, provider failure, tool obsolescence, regulation change, and supply interruption;
11. **capacity and readiness** — availability, mobilization time, resource limits, and current blockers;
12. **evolution and lineage** — versions, predecessors, successors, supporting knowledge, and degraded or obsolete constituents.

### 5.6 A capability declaration is a Claim

```text
provider declaration
≠ capability exists
≠ capability is current
≠ capability applies to this scenario
≠ capability has sufficient capacity
≠ capability is authorized
≠ capability will succeed
```

Capability requires Evidence and qualified Assessment. Historical success is condition-bound. Ten successful domestic transactions do not by themselves establish capability for a defense-sensitive acquisition involving multiple foreign-investment regimes, protected data, sanctions exposure, and substantial antitrust risk.

### 5.7 Capability composition

Capabilities may compose into larger capabilities, but constituent qualification never proves the whole:

```text
qualified constituent capabilities
+ compatible interfaces and authority
+ coordinated execution
+ preserved cross-cutting constraints
+ sufficient combined Evidence
≠ automatically qualified parent capability
```

A cross-border merger capability may depend on antitrust, securities, tax, financing, privacy, labor, sanctions, national-security, diligence, negotiation, litigation, and program-coordination capabilities. Their independent existence does not prove that the firm can integrate them coherently.

Capability composition creates its own recomposition obligation: cross-cutting Intent, privilege, timing, risk, authority, and Evidence must remain coherent across the participating professional systems.

### 5.8 Capability evolution

Conceptually, capability may be hypothesized, assembled, evaluated, qualified, exercised, constrained, degraded, suspended, requalified, evolved, superseded, retired, or reconstituted. These are not proposed canonical states.

Capability can degrade when:

- key professionals leave;
- tacit knowledge is not transferred;
- a supplier or institution disappears;
- a license or authority expires;
- a model, tool, format, or facility becomes obsolete;
- regulation or the scenario class changes;
- a PWA is found defective;
- outcome Evidence exposes systematic failure;
- critical coordination relationships dissolve;
- Narrative Memory or underlying Evidence becomes unavailable or untrustworthy.

This is why capability must be actively preserved. The continued existence of artifacts does not establish the continued existence of the ability that produced them.

### 5.9 Professional Capability portfolio

> A Professional Capability portfolio is a governed, time-qualified projection of the capabilities a Professional System claims, supplies, depends upon, is developing, or no longer possesses, together with their scope, providers, Evidence, Assessments, readiness, capacity, dependencies, and limitations.

It is not a service catalog or marketing inventory. A portfolio entry may be qualified, provisional, constrained, unavailable, degraded, externally supplied, or disputed. The portfolio must distinguish the stable definition of a capability from provider Claims, current operational readiness, and Evidence from particular Undertakings.

At organizational scale, the portfolio helps leaders understand what the institution can responsibly promise. At ecosystem scale, it reveals capability dependencies and gaps across multiple organizations. At civilizational scale, it helps expose concentration, succession, knowledge-loss, and reconstitution risk without presuming that any one party owns the whole capability.

## 6. The relationship between Scenario, Capability, and the current Janumi model

The conceptual relationship is:

> A Professional Scenario expresses situated professional demand. A Professional Capability expresses conditional professional ability. A Professional System realizes that ability. Shape Engineering creates or evolves PWAs that organize its application. An Undertaking commits selected capability to a specific scenario. PWUs bound and coordinate the work. RPH maintains execution coherence. Assurance Engineering controls justified advancement. Outcomes update the scenario and provide Evidence about capability.

The established reusable-to-concrete chain remains:

```text
PWA version
→ owns recursively composed PWU Types
→ is selected for or bound to an Undertaking under accepted compatibility rules

Undertaking
→ instantiates applicable PWU Types as concrete PWU Instances
→ organized in its Professional Work Graph
→ performed through Plans, Workflows, Steps, and Attempts
→ governed through Claims, Evidence, Assurance, Decisions, and Baselines
```

Professional Scenario and Professional Capability do not replace or collapse that chain.

The relationships are many-to-many:

- one scenario may require many capabilities;
- one capability may apply to many scenario classes;
- one capability may employ several PWAs;
- one PWA may require several capabilities;
- one scenario may sponsor multiple Undertakings;
- one Undertaking may contribute to several scenario concerns;
- multiple professional systems may federate to provide one capability.

### 6.1 Demand, supply, and governed resolution

```text
Professional Scenario
→ required-capability analysis
→ capability demand and gaps
→ eligible Professional Systems and capability providers
→ conflict, authority, qualification, policy, capacity, and assurance checks
→ selected capability bindings
→ one or more Undertakings
→ PWU execution and Assurance
→ Outcomes, scenario reconciliation, and capability Evidence
```

The PWA defines required professional work. Humans, agents, tools, organizations, and services provide capability to perform it. A registry declaration may aid discovery, but policy-governed resolution determines eligibility. Execution success does not prove capability or authorize downstream effect.

### 6.2 RPH and Assurance Engineering

RPH is the coordination and control architecture that preserves coherence during recursive execution. Assurance Engineering is the evidence-bearing discipline that determines whether material transformations and professional Claims are sufficiently justified to advance.

At the higher level envisioned here:

- RPH coordinates Undertakings and PWUs within scenario-aware boundaries;
- Assurance Engineering evaluates scenario transformations, work results, recomposition, capability Claims, capability composition, and capability health;
- Governance retains authority to approve, waive where permitted, accept risk, promote Baselines, qualify capabilities, and authorize learning;
- no scenario, PWA, capability declaration, agent, Validator, or registry grants itself authority.

## 7. Exemplar: a consequential international merger

Consider an elite international law firm representing **Orion Systems**, a publicly traded American defense-and-cloud company proposing an eighteen-billion-dollar cash-and-stock acquisition of **Helix Robotics**, a European industrial-automation and AI company.

The apparent transaction becomes a complex professional system:

- Helix operates in the EU, UK, United States, China, Singapore, and Israel;
- its robotics technology has military and civilian applications and may be export-controlled;
- Orion performs classified government work, creating national-security concerns;
- the transaction requires competition review in multiple jurisdictions;
- foreign-investment, foreign-subsidy, sanctions, and data-transfer regimes may apply;
- a Chinese subsidiary holds industrial and employee data that cannot simply enter a United States diligence environment;
- a former distributor may have sold equipment to a sanctioned customer;
- a whistleblower alleges a concealed cybersecurity incident;
- a patent claimant threatens an injunction against a core product line;
- European works councils possess consultation rights;
- acquisition financing expires if closing does not occur within twelve months;
- a rival bidder appears after signing;
- transaction information leaks, creating possible securities-disclosure and insider-control obligations;
- regulators propose remedies that may require divesting the business Orion considers strategically essential;
- the parties must avoid unlawful information exchange and gun-jumping while evaluating integration feasibility.

This is a Professional Scenario because it is one evolving, consequential situation whose relevant state cannot be reduced to a deal checklist or one legal workstream.

### 7.1 Scenario structure

The scenario relates:

- the commercial and strategic Intent of the parties;
- corporate, regulatory, employee, customer, supplier, and public interests;
- known, disputed, and inaccessible facts;
- possible deal structures and future states;
- financing, timing, disclosure, and closing constraints;
- alternative regulatory remedies;
- privilege and confidentiality compartments;
- historical Decisions and effective terms;
- emerging Evidence and external Events;
- linked Undertakings and their cross-effects.

It is not a common room in which every participant can see everything. The principal deal team, antitrust clean team, national-security team, employment lawyers, local counsel, bankers, economists, executives, and board members have different legitimate views.

### 7.2 Capability demand

The scenario may require capabilities in:

- cross-border public-company M&A;
- global antitrust and merger control;
- national-security and foreign-investment review;
- export controls and sanctions;
- securities regulation and disclosure;
- financing and tax structuring;
- intellectual-property and technology diligence;
- privacy, cybersecurity, and international data transfer;
- employment and works-council consultation;
- regulatory-remedy analysis;
- transactional drafting and negotiation;
- complex litigation and regulatory appeal;
- closing and post-merger integration governance;
- privilege, confidentiality, conflicts, and ethical-wall management.

No single lawyer, agent, PWA, or organization necessarily supplies all of them. The firm's Professional Capability portfolio expresses candidate supply, but scenario-specific resolution must still consider jurisdiction, qualification, conflicts, authority, capacity, Evidence, and secure access.

### 7.3 Multiple Undertakings

The scenario might sponsor distinct but related Undertakings for:

1. transaction feasibility and structure;
2. legal and regulatory due diligence;
3. global competition review;
4. national-security review;
5. financing and tax;
6. transaction-document negotiation;
7. disclosure and insider controls;
8. cybersecurity incident investigation;
9. regulator engagement and remedy design;
10. closing readiness and post-merger integration;
11. litigation and failed-transaction contingencies.

Each Undertaking may select an appropriate PWA version and own its PWU Instances. The scenario supplies evolving context and preserves cross-Undertaking consequences without turning the entire engagement into one monolithic work graph.

### 7.4 Continuous reconciliation

Suppose a regulator demands a remedy that divests the target's autonomous-systems division.

That Event may:

- undermine the client's approved strategic Intent;
- invalidate valuation and financing assumptions;
- reopen tax and transaction-structure work;
- change securities-disclosure obligations;
- alter employee consultation requirements;
- affect national-security risk;
- change the integration architecture;
- create a new possible future in which the client abandons the transaction.

The system should not merely append a regulatory document. It should preserve the Event, qualify the source, determine affected Claims and Assumptions, invalidate or supersede dependent state, initiate governed successor work, and present authorized actors with the changed decision landscape.

This is scenario-aware professional coherence, not document automation.

## 8. Narrative Memory and governed capability learning

Narrative Memory is the interpretive bridge between professional experience and future capability. It preserves the meaning of a trajectory without replacing the canonical objects, Events, Evidence, or Decisions from which that interpretation is derived.

### 8.1 Scenario Narrative Memory

A scenario narrative explains:

- how the situation was understood at different times;
- what participants knew or could lawfully access;
- what changed;
- why Decisions were made;
- which alternatives were considered or rejected;
- how uncertainty evolved;
- what remained disputed, missing, or inaccessible;
- what consequences followed.

It is a provenance-linked interpretation, not a retrospective rewriting of history.

### 8.2 Capability Narrative Memory

A capability narrative explains:

- how the capability was assembled and qualified;
- which scenarios exercised it;
- what repeatedly succeeded or failed;
- which Assumptions proved fragile;
- how PWAs, methods, policies, and providers evolved;
- where tacit knowledge and critical dependencies emerged;
- why capability was constrained, degraded, requalified, superseded, or retired.

It is the longitudinal account of capability evolution, not a Boolean reputation score.

### 8.3 Isolation classes

Narrative Memory must preserve the authority and confidentiality of its sources. At minimum, the vision distinguishes:

1. **Scenario- or matter-private memory** — the privileged or confidential account of one scenario;
2. **organization-private capability memory** — governed learning available within an authorized institution;
3. **consortium or federation memory** — selectively shared learning under explicit multi-party governance;
4. **shareable professional knowledge** — public, licensed, client-authorized, or sufficiently abstracted knowledge approved for broader use;
5. **civilizational knowledge** — enduring knowledge and practice stewarded across institutions and generations.

Movement among these classes is a protected transformation, not automatic training.

### 8.4 Governed learning path

```text
Undertaking experience and observed Outcomes
→ scenario-specific reflection
→ candidate capability lesson
→ privilege, confidentiality, consent, and leakage review
→ representativeness and causal-limitations review
→ independent assurance
→ authorized capability, PWA, policy, or technique revision
→ new version and requalification
```

A successful or failed engagement can inform future capability without silently exposing protected details or granting a model permission to rewrite authoritative professional policy.

No later abstraction erases the source history. No generalized lesson becomes professional truth merely because it was learned frequently. Outcome correlation does not prove causation, and aggregate learning can still disclose sensitive information.

## 9. Time, continuity, and trajectory

Time is a first-class dimension of Professional Scenario, Professional Capability, Narrative Memory, Evidence, authority, and civilizational stewardship.

### 9.1 Multiple professional times

Janumi must be able to distinguish conceptually:

- when something occurred;
- when it was observed;
- when an actor learned it;
- when it was recorded;
- when a Decision became effective;
- when a Claim or policy was valid;
- when Evidence was fresh enough to support a conclusion;
- when work was performed;
- when an Outcome became observable;
- when capability was qualified, available, degraded, or restored.

A legal recommendation must be evaluated against the law, Evidence, instructions, and information legitimately available when it was made—not against facts learned months later.

### 9.2 Capability trajectory

```text
capability at time t0
→ exercised in historical scenarios
→ produces Outcomes and Evidence
→ knowledge, people, institutions, and technology change
→ capability matures, branches, degrades, or enters dormancy
→ successor capability at time t1
→ transferred across organizations and generations
→ re-exercised under new conditions
```

Continuity may therefore be lineage rather than literal sameness. A modern semiconductor capability is not identical to its predecessor forty years ago, yet its evolution may remain traceable through knowledge, institutions, tooling, standards, and professional practice.

### 9.3 Temporal questions

For any capability Claim, Janumi should ultimately help answer:

```text
Which capability version existed?
During what period and under what conditions?
What Evidence supported the Claim at that time?
Who could provide it and with what authority?
What knowledge, infrastructure, and dependencies were available?
What had become stale, obsolete, inaccessible, or invalid?
How quickly could the capability be mobilized?
Over what horizon were its Outcomes evaluated?
What changed after the last successful exercise?
```

### 9.4 Path dependence and irreversibility

Professional systems inherit the consequences of prior investment, institutional design, standards, training, infrastructure, and Decisions. Some paths can be reversed only at great cost; others cannot be recovered once knowledge, biodiversity, trust, infrastructure, or human expertise is lost.

The vision of Janumi is therefore not only to optimize the next Action. It is to help professionals understand which present Decisions preserve, foreclose, or create future capability.

## 10. Civilizational Capability

### 10.1 Candidate definition

> A Civilizational Capability is a durable, distributed, and intergenerational ability of a societal ecosystem to produce, sustain, adapt, or reconstitute a consequential class of Outcomes across time, despite turnover or discontinuity in its people, organizations, institutions, knowledge custodians, infrastructure, and technologies.

Civilizational Capability is the ultimate scale expression of the same underlying theory. It is not merely a very large company capability.

### 10.2 Defining characteristics

#### No single owner

Civilizational capabilities emerge from ecosystems of firms, governments, universities, standards bodies, regulators, professions, suppliers, communities, archives, infrastructure operators, and generations of practitioners.

No participant possesses all necessary knowledge or authority. Janumi must therefore support federation, not presume centralized ownership.

#### Partial and heterogeneous knowledge

Relevant knowledge may be:

- scientific and public;
- proprietary;
- privileged or classified;
- regulated;
- licensed;
- tacit;
- embodied in equipment;
- embedded in organizational routines;
- preserved through apprenticeship;
- dependent on language, culture, and trust;
- known only through long-standing relationships.

Janumi may help externalize and connect capability, but it must never assume that all professional knowledge can or should be captured.

#### Intergenerational identity

A civilizational capability persists while people retire, organizations merge, technologies become obsolete, standards change, geopolitical conditions shift, and responsibilities move between humans and agents.

Capability identity cannot therefore be tied to one provider, artifact, PWA, model, toolchain, or technology generation.

#### Federation and plural authority

At civilizational scale, there may be no single authoritative scenario. Participants maintain different authorized projections:

- scientific;
- public-policy;
- industrial;
- commercial;
- regulatory;
- national-security;
- labor and community;
- environmental and intergenerational.

These views may overlap, conflict, conceal information, and operate under different legal authority. Janumi's long-term architecture must favor selective disclosure, provenance-bearing attestations, shared interfaces, negotiated Baselines, and cross-authority reconciliation over a universal central database.

#### Dormancy and reconstitution

Continuity does not require uninterrupted operation. A civilization may lose the immediate ability to perform a practice while retaining enough knowledge, institutions, infrastructure, and lineage to recover it.

Reconstitution requires more than archived documents. It may require rebuilding supply chains, training practitioners, restoring facilities, emulating obsolete tools, recovering tacit practices, and re-establishing professional legitimacy.

### 10.3 Capability health

Civilizational capability stewardship would observe:

- workforce and apprenticeship pipelines;
- key-person and sole-custodian knowledge risk;
- supplier and geographic concentration;
- critical infrastructure and maintenance capacity;
- format, tool, and technology obsolescence;
- institutional continuity and legitimacy;
- regulatory authority;
- resilience to disaster and geopolitical interruption;
- substitution and recovery paths;
- ability to reproduce prior results;
- ability to adapt to novel scenarios;
- knowledge transfer across generations;
- the difference between owning artifacts and retaining the capability to recreate them.

### 10.4 Civilizational manifestations

An EUV lithography machine is a physical manifestation of capabilities in plasma physics, optics, materials, metrology, control software, precision manufacturing, supply coordination, field service, finance, regulation, and decades of accumulated scientific and industrial knowledge.

CERN is a manifestation of capabilities in physics, cryogenics, large-scale infrastructure, international governance, scientific collaboration, detector engineering, distributed computing, and long-horizon institutional commitment.

The James Webb Space Telescope and Starship similarly manifest professional ecosystems whose capabilities survive individual artifacts and participants.

If every existing artifact disappeared, the decisive question would be whether the professional system retained enough capability to produce another. If the artifacts remained but the system lost the knowledge and coordination to understand, maintain, or reproduce them, the capability would already be in decline.

### 10.5 Stewardship, not centralized control

Janumi should not aspire to control civilization. It should aspire to make professional capability more explicit, inspectable, evidence-backed, composable, resilient, transferable, and capable of governed learning.

The appropriate long-term term is **stewardship**:

> Janumi provides infrastructure for the governed stewardship of professional capability across changing scenarios, organizations, technologies, authorities, and generations of time.

## 11. Shape Engineering as the practical engineering discipline

The civilizational vision does not change where Janumi should begin.

Shape Engineering is the discipline of discovering, modeling, validating, encoding, and evolving the executable shape of professional work. It converts professional understanding into reusable architectures whose obligations, Evidence, authority, assurance, and recomposition can be inspected and tested.

It asks:

> Given a class of consequential scenarios and intended Outcomes, how should professional capability be organized so that humans and agents can act coherently and reliably?

PWAs are the principal executable products of that discipline. PWU Types provide recursively bounded professional obligations. Undertakings instantiate them against concrete Intent. RPH coordinates their execution. Assurance Engineering determines whether work remains justified.

This is the right commercialization layer because it offers immediate, demonstrable value:

```text
desired professional Outcome
→ Shape Engineering
→ governed PWA
→ human-agent execution
→ assured professional result
→ observed Outcome
```

Every successful Shape Engineering engagement may also produce carefully governed Evidence about broader Professional Capability. The near-term business and long-term vision can therefore reinforce one another without requiring a speculative pivot.

JanumiCode is the first demanding existence proof. If Shape Engineering can reliably outperform prompt engineering, ad hoc agent orchestration, and artifact-centered automation in software engineering, the method becomes credible for additional professional domains.

## 12. Domain expressions of one universal grammar

Janumi's semantics are intended to be universal, but universal does not mean domainless or all-knowing. The universal layer supplies a grammar for professional work; domain ontologies and PWAs supply professional meaning.

### 12.1 JanumiCode

Software engineering emphasizes recursive decomposition, semantic preservation, architecture, implementation, verification, release, and continuing operational observation.

JanumiCode demonstrates that an agent producing code is not equivalent to a product obligation being satisfied. It provides the proving ground for PWA authoring, recursive work, Assurance Engineering, Narrative Memory, and governed capability learning.

### 12.2 JanumiConstruction

Construction emphasizes physically coupled dependencies, distributed organizations, federated repositories, readiness, inspections, changing field conditions, and protected release of downstream work.

It reveals that much of what professionals call administration is actually the imperfect control machinery by which complex work maintains coherence:

```text
RFI          → ambiguity control
submittal    → conformance control
schedule     → dependency representation
inspection   → professional Evidence and gate input
daily report → observation of actual state
change order → governed reconstitution of agreement
```

Janumi's opportunity is not to automate away these purposes. It is to preserve them more coherently across fragmented systems and changing reality.

### 12.3 JanumiHealth

Healthcare emphasizes longitudinal Evidence, competing hypotheses, missing information, explanation coverage, disconfirmation, uncertainty, specialist authority, privacy, and patient safety.

JanumiHealth's compelling initial form is not an autonomous diagnostician. It is a persistent, evidence-governed investigation of an unresolved case whose output helps qualified clinicians act with a more complete and challengeable representation.

It tests whether Janumi can resist premature convergence and preserve what remains unexplained across years of fragmented encounters.

### 12.4 JanumiLegal

Law emphasizes interpretation, precedent, adversarial positions, privilege, client authority, jurisdiction, changing facts, institutional decision-making, ethical walls, and the distinction between advice and authorized legal action.

The international-merger scenario demonstrates how a Professional Scenario may span multiple PWAs and Undertakings while preserving compartmented knowledge and cross-workstream consequences.

### 12.5 Different topology, common primitive

```text
software      → recursive decomposition and recomposition
legal         → interpretation, argument, authority, and compartmentation
healthcare    → longitudinal investigation and hypothesis revision
construction  → distributed coordination and physical-state reconciliation
```

No domain is exclusively one topology. The lesson is that PWUs may remain a common primitive while the profession determines how they compose, depend, iterate, acquire Evidence, and exercise authority.

## 13. Capability supply: humans, agents, skills, tools, and registries

Janumi defines professional work independently of who or what performs it.

The capability supply layer may include:

- qualified human professionals;
- organizational service providers;
- specialist agents;
- agent runtimes;
- deterministic tools;
- professional techniques and skill packages;
- external data and execution services;
- Validator implementations;
- facilities and physical infrastructure.

### 13.1 Work demand is not capability supply

```text
PWA and PWU Type
→ define required professional work

capability provider
→ declares what it may be able to perform

governed resolution
→ determines eligibility for an exact scenario and work instance

runtime binding
→ authorizes a bounded Attempt

Assurance Engineering
→ evaluates the material result
```

An agent, human, or organization may claim capability. That declaration supports discovery but does not prove qualification, applicability, readiness, independence, or authority.

### 13.2 Useful capability distinctions

A disciplined supply layer distinguishes:

- a **tool**, which performs a bounded operation;
- a **technique or skill package**, which extends how a provider works;
- an **agent capability provider**, which may possess independent reasoning, identity, lifecycle, and deployment;
- a **human or organizational provider**, which contributes competence and professional authority;
- a **Validator implementation**, which evaluates a bounded Assurance Policy concern;
- a **PWA**, which defines professional work rather than supplying an executor;
- an **application**, which provides a user or system experience around these capabilities.

The Google Atomic Agents discussion is useful because it articulates capability packaging, registries, discovery, governed gateways, versioning, and reusable evaluation. Those ideas fit beneath Janumi's work and assurance model.

They must not collapse Janumi's boundaries:

```text
Atomic Agent ≠ PWU Type or PWU Instance
agent blueprint ≠ PWA
agent execution graph ≠ Professional Work Graph
Agent Card ≠ capability proof
metric or evaluation ≠ Assurance Assessment
registry search ≠ trustworthy provider selection
guardrail ≠ Assurance Engineering
```

### 13.3 Governed resolution

Once multiple providers claim the same capability, selection becomes a control problem. Resolution may consider:

- required capability and outcome contract;
- input/output and semantic compatibility;
- PWA/profile compatibility;
- domain and jurisdictional applicability;
- authorization and data-access eligibility;
- approved provider and version;
- qualification and performance Evidence;
- independence and common-mode risk;
- availability, health, latency, and cost;
- region, residency, and network constraints;
- capacity and mobilization time;
- fallback, recovery, and escalation.

Resolution itself may be material professional or control state. It must be inspectable and must not become a magical self-optimizing mesh whose choices cannot be reconstructed or challenged.

### 13.4 Product and service tiers

The capability-supply model supports differentiated delivery without fragmenting core semantics.

#### Open-source Janumi

The open-source tier should provide:

- portable semantic and capability-package contracts;
- local registries or catalogs;
- local and self-hosted execution adapters;
- explicit or deterministic provider binding;
- PWA, PWU, RPH, and Assurance Engineering foundations;
- full de minimis assurance and durable Evidence;
- interoperable tool and agent protocols;
- version pinning, provenance, and conformance tests.

Core correctness and trustworthy assurance must not be reserved for enterprise customers.

#### Managed and freemium Janumi services

Managed services may provide:

- hosted catalogs and community packages;
- curated PWAs, techniques, agents, and Validator implementations;
- managed runtimes and storage;
- guided authoring and capability matching;
- standard evaluation and observability;
- compatibility analysis;
- convenient deployment and collaboration;
- bounded managed learning features.

#### Enterprise Janumi

Enterprise services may provide:

- private and federated registries;
- enterprise identity, delegation, and gateways;
- organization-specific ontology and policy;
- data residency and network controls;
- approved provider and model pools;
- private/hybrid runtimes;
- high-assurance capability resolution;
- independent Validator portfolios;
- SLO-, health-, cost-, and risk-aware routing;
- canarying, promotion, rollback, and suspension;
- large connector ecosystems;
- multi-organization and multi-authority federation;
- advanced audit, retention, legal hold, and compliance.

Paid tiers monetize managed scale, curation, integration, federation, and organizational governance—not permission to be trustworthy.

## 14. Universal semantics, domain meaning, and Platform services

The Janumi architecture separates semantic meaning from its technical realization.

| Layer | Responsibility |
|---|---|
| **Universal Janumi semantics** | Express professional identity, Intent, Outcomes, work, obligation, Claims, Evidence, authority, assurance, reconciliation, provenance, time, and lifecycle meaning. |
| **Domain ontology and PWAs** | Define professional meaning in software, law, healthcare, construction, engineering, and other domains. |
| **Janumi Platform services** | Persist, secure, execute, connect, observe, and enforce the semantic model. |
| **Customer and federation configuration** | Define organizational identity, compartments, private registries, approved providers, local policy, deployment topology, and contractual relationships. |

### 14.1 Universal does not mean domainless

Universal semantics provide a grammar, not every professional noun.

Janumi Core may understand that:

- an object has identity, provenance, version, authority, and access scope;
- a Claim may be supported or contradicted by Evidence;
- an Assumption can become invalid;
- a Decision requires authority;
- work has obligations and recomposition;
- assurance protects a transition;
- changed reality may require reconciliation.

A legal domain extension defines merger filings, privilege, clean teams, regulator requests, legal opinions, and remedies. A healthcare extension defines symptoms, biomarkers, hypotheses, interventions, consent, and clinician authority. A construction extension defines submittals, RFIs, inspections, site conditions, and release gates.

Domain semantics extend the universal grammar; they do not replace it.

### 14.2 Platform services realize rather than redefine

Universal semantics may require that every object be authority-scoped. Domain policy may require attorney-client privilege. A law firm configures a matter-specific ethical wall. Platform services enforce the resulting rule through identity, policy evaluation, encryption, storage isolation, query controls, redaction, and audit.

The Platform implements the invariant. It does not define the professional meaning of privilege or authority.

### 14.3 Portability of meaning

Janumi's long-term value depends on semantic portability across deployment models and changing technology.

A PWA, Scenario, capability Claim, Assessment, or Narrative Memory should not derive its meaning from a particular cloud service, model provider, database, agent framework, or visualization. Platform bindings may change while the professional contract remains interpretable.

## 15. Isolation, delegation, federation, and multi-authority governance

The ultimate vision requires these properties to be designed from the core, even when early deployments are simpler.

### 15.1 Tenant isolation is necessary but insufficient

A law-firm tenant contains mutually restricted clients, matters, workstreams, clean teams, and privilege classes. A healthcare tenant contains patient, care-team, consent, research, and regulatory boundaries. A construction program contains owner, designer, contractor, subcontractor, regulator, insurer, and supplier boundaries.

Access may depend on:

```text
tenant
+ organization
+ client or subject
+ scenario or matter
+ workstream and compartment
+ professional purpose
+ role and authority
+ jurisdiction
+ time
+ consent and contractual terms
+ information classification
```

### 15.2 Human and agent identity

Humans and their agent proxies require separate identities. An agent delegation should be bounded by:

- delegating principal;
- agent and runtime identity;
- scenario, Undertaking, PWU, matter, and compartment;
- professional purpose;
- permitted data and operations;
- allowed tools and network destinations;
- effective and expiration times;
- onward-disclosure restrictions;
- Evidence and logging requirements;
- assurance and independence requirements;
- explicit prohibitions on professional Decisions or external Actions.

Authentication, technical authorization, professional authority, ownership, and assurance remain distinct. Access to privileged material does not authorize an agent to waive privilege, approve a filing, prescribe treatment, accept a construction inspection, or bind an organization.

### 15.3 Historical affiliations

Organizational relationships change across time. Prior membership in a team must remain part of history without granting current access. Joining an organization later must not automatically disclose historical matters. Termination of access must not erase the audit record or the professional meaning of prior authorized Actions.

### 15.4 Federation without centralization

At ecosystem and civilizational scale, Janumi should support cooperation among independently governed systems through:

- typed capability and work contracts;
- selective disclosure;
- provenance-bearing attestations;
- shared but scoped Baselines;
- verifiable identities and authority;
- negotiated policy and interface versions;
- compartment-preserving projections;
- explicit disagreement and unresolved state;
- local custody of protected Evidence;
- durable cross-system correlation and reconciliation.

Federation should not require every participant to surrender data or authority to a central Janumi operator.

## 16. Strategic evolution horizons

These horizons express direction, not promised dates or implementation phases. Progress may be iterative, overlapping, and conditional on Evidence, customers, resources, and governance.

### Horizon 1 — Prove Shape Engineering

Demonstrate in JanumiCode that explicit PWA/PWU/RPH/Assurance architecture produces better professional Outcomes than prompt engineering, chat-only agents, and conventional orchestration.

Success requires measurable improvement in Intent preservation, completeness, defect escape, rework, Evidence quality, recovery, and human trust—not merely larger graphs or more agent activity.

### Horizon 2 — Operationalize reusable professional capability supply

Establish portable packages, typed catalogs, provider declarations, governed resolution, versioning, compatibility, independent assurance, and delivery across open-source, managed, and enterprise environments.

The provider remains distinct from the work contract.

### Horizon 3 — Introduce Professional Scenario as an explicit coordination context

Enable selected domain implementations to maintain evolving scenario state across multiple Undertakings, repositories, authorities, and possible futures.

Start with bounded reference scenarios whose value is observable, such as a complex product realization, construction work package, legal matter, or unresolved clinical investigation.

### Horizon 4 — Expand domain capability families

Develop JanumiLegal, JanumiConstruction, JanumiHealth, and other domains through specialized ontologies, PWAs, assurance policies, reference Undertakings, professional partnerships, and rigorous authority boundaries.

Each domain should validate or challenge the universal model rather than merely apply different labels to a software workflow.

### Horizon 5 — Govern capability portfolios and organizational learning

Represent capability Claims, composition, qualification, readiness, performance, degradation, and lineage. Use Narrative Memory and observed Outcomes to propose improvements while preserving privacy, privilege, provenance, and human/governance authority.

Learning remains versioned, inspectable, reversible, and subject to requalification.

### Horizon 6 — Federate professional systems

Support selective, policy-governed collaboration among organizations that retain independent data, authority, and infrastructure. Enable shared scenario concerns and composite capabilities without assuming one owner or universal view.

### Horizon 7 — Steward civilizational capability across time

Help professional ecosystems observe capability health, knowledge continuity, succession, dependency concentration, adaptation, degradation, and reconstitution across technologies, organizations, institutions, and generations.

This horizon is a directional north star, not a product claim. Its purpose is to ensure that near-term architecture does not foreclose long-term stewardship.

## 17. What Janumi must never become

The larger the vision, the more important its limits.

Janumi must not become:

1. **An omniscient model of reality.** It contains partial, qualified representations and observations.
2. **A universal central database.** Protected knowledge and authority remain federated.
3. **A system that treats artifacts as Outcomes.** Outputs remain subordinate to professional and real-world purpose.
4. **A workflow engine disguised as professional theory.** Temporal execution is only one layer.
5. **A graph that confuses relationship types.** Composition, dependency, authority, Evidence, progression, and execution remain distinct.
6. **An agent-centric ontology.** Humans, institutions, physical systems, and authority remain constitutive.
7. **A self-authorizing professional actor.** Agents, Validators, scenarios, and capability registries cannot grant professional authority.
8. **A machine for concealing uncertainty.** Missing Evidence, contradictions, limitations, and residual risk remain visible.
9. **A silent cross-client or cross-tenant learning system.** Protected experience never becomes shared capability without explicit authority and assurance.
10. **A system that freezes bureaucracy.** Janumi preserves the professional purpose of controls, not every historical form.
11. **A capability marketplace based on self-description.** Provider Claims require qualification and current Evidence.
12. **A system that optimizes local success while destroying future capability.** Path dependence and long-horizon effects remain part of professional reasoning.
13. **A replacement for professional judgment or democratic and institutional governance.** It supports accountable judgment; it does not abolish it.
14. **An assurance theater of scores and green badges.** Every material control remains bound, executed, recorded, inspectable, and enforced.
15. **A claim to capture all human or tacit knowledge.** Knowledge boundaries and loss risk must be acknowledged.

## 18. Strategic hypotheses and evidence obligations

This vision should remain challengeable. Its central hypotheses include:

1. Professional work can be represented through a universal semantic grammar without erasing domain topology.
2. Explicit recursive work and recomposition preserve Intent better than task decomposition alone.
3. Invocation-level and compositional Assurance Engineering reduce plausible-but-wrong professional output.
4. Persistent scenario representation improves coordination across changing Evidence, repositories, and authorities.
5. Evidence-backed capability portfolios improve provider selection, resilience, succession, and organizational learning.
6. Narrative Memory can improve future professional performance without becoming a competing source of truth.
7. Governed learning can accumulate capability without violating tenant isolation, privilege, consent, or provenance.
8. Human-agent professional systems can outperform chat-only assistance and autonomous-agent orchestration on long-horizon work.
9. The same underlying model can support software, law, construction, healthcare, and other professions while preserving their differences.
10. Near-term Shape Engineering can create commercial value while building toward long-term professional capability stewardship.

These hypotheses require empirical demonstration through reference Undertakings, conformance tests, customer Outcomes, failure analysis, escaped-defect observation, longitudinal studies, and professional review.

The vision must be willing to learn that a proposed abstraction is unnecessary, incomplete, unsafe, or wrongly bounded. No theory becomes true because it is ambitious.

## 19. The long-term thesis

Human civilization advances because professional systems preserve and extend the ability to understand, decide, build, heal, govern, maintain, and learn.

Those abilities are fragile. They can be fragmented across organizations, concealed in tacit practice, lost through turnover, weakened by obsolete infrastructure, distorted by incentives, interrupted by catastrophe, or rendered incoherent by technological and institutional change.

AI greatly expands the amount of professional activity that can be attempted. It does not automatically preserve Intent, truth, authority, wholeness, or capability. Without a stronger architecture, increased generation may produce increased fragmentation.

Janumi's opportunity is to provide that architecture.

It begins with Shape Engineering: making professional work explicit enough that humans and agents can perform it coherently.

It deepens through Assurance Engineering: making justified advancement, failure, uncertainty, and authority durable and inspectable.

It expands through Professional Scenarios: preserving the changing situations to which work must respond.

It matures through Professional Capability: understanding what professional systems can actually accomplish, under what conditions, and with what Evidence.

It learns through Narrative Memory: preserving the meaning of experience without erasing provenance or confidentiality.

It reaches its ultimate expression in Civilizational Capability: helping societies sustain, adapt, transfer, and reconstitute the abilities on which future generations depend.

The grand aspiration is therefore:

> Janumi seeks to help humanity move from producing isolated professional artifacts to deliberately stewarding professional capability—across complex scenarios, across institutions, across technological eras, and across time.

That vision is larger than any initial product. It is also continuous with the work already begun.

JanumiCode is the proof. Shape Engineering is the entry point. The Professional Workbench is the environment. RPH is the coordination architecture. Assurance Engineering is the control fabric. Professional Scenario is the situated context. Professional Capability is the enduring asset. Civilizational stewardship is the horizon.
